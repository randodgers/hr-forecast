Methodology

Each hitter's power rate blends his home-run rate against the opposing starter's handedness (platoon split, regressed) with a barrel-rate-based expected HR rate from Statcast. That rate is then adjusted by four multipliers: last-10-games form (hot/cold, heavily regressed), the starter's HR allowed vs. this batter's side, the ballpark's 3-year Statcast HR park factor, and a weather factor built from first-pitch temperature, out-blowing wind relative to the stadium's orientation, and air pressure. Game probability assumes 3.7–4.7 plate appearances by lineup spot. The HRFI is the weather factor alone mapped onto a 1–10 scale; domes score a flat 5.

The Market column shows each sportsbook price converted to an implied probability. That number still includes the book's vig, so it overstates the true market probability by a few points; Edge (model % − market %) is therefore a conservative comparison. Prices refresh with the data, not in real time.
